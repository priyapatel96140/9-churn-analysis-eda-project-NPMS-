# Business Recommendations

## 1. Encourage Long-Term Contracts

Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate. Telecom companies should encourage customers to switch to one-year or two-year contracts by offering discounts, loyalty rewards, or exclusive benefits.


## 2. Improve Customer Retention During Early Tenure

Customers with lower tenure are more likely to churn. Companies should focus on onboarding programs, welcome offers, and regular customer engagement during the first few months of service.


## 3. Promote Value-Added Services

Customers who do not subscribe to services such as Online Security, Tech Support, Device Protection, and Online Backup tend to churn more frequently. Offering bundled service packages can improve customer satisfaction and reduce churn.


## 4. Review Pricing for High Monthly Charges

Customers paying higher monthly charges are more likely to leave the company. Telecom providers should evaluate their pricing strategies and offer personalized discounts or better-value plans for high-paying customers.


## 5. Improve Customer Support Quality

Poor customer experience is one of the major reasons for churn. Providing faster issue resolution, proactive customer support, and personalized assistance can increase customer loyalty and satisfaction.


## 6. Target High-Risk Customer Segments

Using customer behavior patterns such as contract type, tenure, monthly charges, and subscribed services, companies should identify high-risk customers and launch personalized retention campaigns before they decide to leave.


## 7. Encourage Automatic Payment Methods

Customers using electronic check payment methods often experience higher churn. Promoting automatic payment options such as bank transfers or credit card payments can improve customer retention and reduce payment-related issues.


## 8. Continuously Monitor Customer Churn Trends

Telecom companies should regularly analyze customer behavior, service usage, and churn patterns to identify emerging risks. Continuous monitoring enables businesses to make data-driven decisions and improve long-term customer retention strategies.
